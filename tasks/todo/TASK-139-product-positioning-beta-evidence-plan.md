# TASK-139: Product Positioning and Beta Evidence Plan

**Phase:** Product reset and public beta
**Priority:** high (P1)
**Status:** todo
**Area:** product / research / strategy

## Description

Turn the broad bookmark-manager vision into a testable product thesis. Define
who should switch to Little Imp, why existing tools are insufficient, and what
evidence is required before more feature work is approved.

## Scope

1. Define the primary wedge around private local search for developers' saved
   technical knowledge and coding-agent access.
2. Compare the intended experience against current alternatives such as
   Karakeep, Linkwarden, and Raindrop without starting a feature-parity race.
3. Define the target beta cohort, recruitment channels, screening questions,
   interview script, and import/onboarding session.
4. Replace unbounded PRD metrics with measurable beta hypotheses and targets:
   capture success, extraction success, time-to-first-value, successful search,
   opened-result rate, seven-day reuse, and AI configuration rate.
5. Define a privacy-preserving measurement approach using local summaries,
   opt-in exports, and interviews rather than mandatory telemetry.
6. Record explicit stop, continue, and pivot thresholds.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] One primary user and switching trigger are named precisely.
- [ ] The product wedge and non-goals fit on one page.
- [ ] Each beta metric has a definition, target, collection method, and decision threshold.
- [ ] A 5-10 person beta recruitment and interview plan is ready to execute.
- [ ] No new feature tranche is proposed before beta evidence is reviewed.

## Dependencies

- None. This strategy work can proceed while technical release gates are repaired.

## Notes

- The objective is learning, not producing another long vision document.
